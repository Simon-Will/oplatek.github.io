--- layout: post title: Capture part of screen in Ubuntu date: '2014-06-05T09:29:00.000+02:00' author: Ondrej Platek tags: - printscreen - Ubuntu modified_time: '2014-06-05T09:29:58.018+02:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-1883852367990943689.post-5538179369278964084 blogger_orig_url: http://oplatek.blogspot.com/2014/06/capture-part-of-screen-in-ubuntu.html --- I used to capture the entire screen by pressing Printscreen button which (launches ?whatsoever? program) and saves the entire image into my home folder. After that I used to crop it with gimp.

I just discovered import command which can be invoked from command line and 
modifies your cursor to cross so you can define and later save any rectangular area.

$ import save_to_this_file_any_rectangular_part_of_screen.png

It saves me time for launching gimp, so few seconds at least.
